Project Overview

Design and simulation of a 2×1 MIMO Ultra Wideband antenna for microwave-based breast cancer detection.

This project focuses on the development of a compact Ultra Wideband (UWB) MIMO antenna system for biomedical imaging applications.

The proposed antenna operates in the frequency range of 1–10 GHz and uses microwave imaging techniques to detect variations between normal and abnormal breast tissues.

The antenna design is simulated using openEMS and GNU Octave, with FR4 substrate material and microstrip feed technology to achieve wide bandwidth, good impedance matching, and efficient radiation performance.

Key Features

Major advantages and capabilities of the proposed UWB antenna system.

✓ Ultra Wideband

Operates over a wide frequency range of 1–10 GHz suitable for microwave imaging applications.

✓ High Performance

Designed to achieve good impedance matching, low return loss and efficient radiation characteristics.

MIMO antenna configuration provides improved performance while maintaining a compact structure.

Uses microwave imaging technology for safe and non-invasive breast tissue analysis.

Challenges Faced

  • Optimizing antenna dimensions for desired frequency response.
  • Improving impedance matching across the UWB frequency range.
  • Achieving wide bandwidth with stable radiation characteristics.
  • Validating simulation results using electromagnetic analysis.
